Congratulations to Assistant Professor Vijay K. Shah on receiving an NSF CAREER Award — one of the National Science Foundation’s most prestigious honors for early-career faculty.

Shah’s project, O-DSM: An Open Dynamic Spectrum Management Framework for Enhanced 6G Coexistence, is reimagining how the U.S. manages the radio frequency spectrum — a shared natural resource essential to 5G, 6G and future wireless technologies.

His work will develop intelligent, AI-native frameworks for real-time, secure spectrum sharing while mentoring the next generation of wireless researchers through the NextG Wireless Lab.

A proud moment for the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering and the future of connectivity.

🔗 Learn more about the project and the impact of CAREER awards at NC State: http://ncst.at/ebr550WlVZP

Only one Ph.D. student in the world is selected each year.
This year, it’s Shubham Dhiman, a Ph.D. student in NC State ECE.

Shubham has been awarded the 2025 IEEE Joseph John Suozzi INTELEC Fellowship Award in Power Electronics — one of the highest global honors in the field.

He joins fellow NC State recipient Ankan De, who received the award in 2013 under the guidance of Professor Subhashish Bhattacharya.

We’re proud to see NC State’s leadership in power electronics recognized on the world stage.
