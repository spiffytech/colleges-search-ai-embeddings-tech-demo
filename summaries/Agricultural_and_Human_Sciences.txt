Agricultural and Human Sciences integrates research, education, and extension to strengthen families and communities. Our unique focus on applied research addresses real-world challenges in food systems, family wellbeing, and community development.

The Gore Research Kitchens provide state-of-the-art facilities for nutrition and food science innovation. Our LEAP Teaching Licensure Program prepares educators to address agricultural literacy in K-12 settings.

Extension programs translate research into practical solutions for North Carolina communities. International initiatives foster global perspectives on sustainable agriculture and human development.

As part of NC State's College of Agriculture and Life Sciences, we advance the land-grant mission through research that improves lives and strengthens communities across North Carolina.
